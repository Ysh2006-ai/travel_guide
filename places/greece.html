About Santorini

Santorini, officially known as Thira, is a volcanic island in the Cyclades group of the Greek islands. It is famous for its dramatic views, stunning sunsets, white-washed buildings, and its own active volcano.

The island was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape. The whitewashed, cubiform houses of its two principal towns, Fira and Oia, cling to cliffs above an underwater caldera (crater).

Today, Santorini is one of Greece's most popular tourist destinations, known for its romantic setting, luxury accommodations, and breathtaking views.

Must-See Attractions

Sunset in Oia

Witness the world-famous sunset from the village of Oia, as the sun dips below the horizon painting the sky in spectacular hues of orange and pink.

White-Washed Buildings

Marvel at the iconic architecture of Santorini with its pristine white buildings and blue domes that create a stunning contrast against the azure sea.

Caldera View

Enjoy the breathtaking views of the volcanic caldera, a reminder of the island's dramatic geological history.

Fira Town

Explore the capital of Santorini with its maze-like streets, charming shops, and spectacular cliff-top restaurants.

Culture & Traditions

Santorini's rich cultural heritage is a blend of ancient Greek traditions and the unique local customs that have evolved on this volcanic island.

Local Cuisine

Try local specialties such as fava (yellow split pea puree), tomatokeftedes (tomato fritters), and fresh seafood. Don't miss the chance to taste wines from Santorini's vineyards, which produce unique varieties due to the volcanic soil.

Traditional Crafts

The island maintains traditions of pottery and basket weaving. Visit local workshops to see artisans practicing these age-old crafts.

Festivals

Experience local culture through religious festivals and celebrations held throughout the year, featuring music, dancing, and traditional food.
